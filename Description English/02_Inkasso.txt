# Debt Collection

## INFORMATION FOR CREDITORS (Companies/Customers of Creditreform)

---

### Debt Collection: Faster Debt Recovery and Liquidity Protection

- **Services**  
  - Pre-legal, legal, and judicial debt collection as well as realization of loss certificates  
  - Online portal for submitting outstanding claims  
  - Calculation of recovery chances via [inkassorechner.ch](https://inkassorechner.ch/)

  - [Debt Collection and Receivables Management](https://www.creditreform.ch/loesungen/inkasso-und-forderungsmanagement)

### Successful Dunning Process: Procedure and Benefits

1. **Your Actions**  
   - After unsuccessful invoice and reminder: contact customer by phone  
   - If payment remains outstanding: transfer to Creditreform  

2. **Our Service**  
   - Identification and credit check of debtor  
   - Mediation and payment request, installment agreement, monitoring  

3. **Your Advantage**  
   - Maintain customer relationships  
   - Increase liquidity, reduce emotional stress  
   - Focus on core business, fewer legal steps  
   - Success-based fee: you pay only upon success

### Converting Loss Certificates into Liquidity

1. **Your Actions**  
   - Submit loss certificates or written-off claims, report direct payments  

2. **Our Service**  
   - Persistent management of your loss certificates  
   - Prevention of statute of limitations (20-year period)  

3. **Your Advantage**  
   - No personnel effort or cost risk  
   - Increase in extraordinary income  
   - Fee only upon success  

**Note**: Loss certificates expire 20 years after issuance (SchKG revision 1997).

### CrediCAP Debt Collection Insurance for SMEs

1. **Known Challenge**  
   - Objections complicate debt recovery  

2. **Solution**  
   - Insurance covering court and attorney fees in case of objections  
   - Exclusively for Creditreform members  

3. **Conditions**  
   - Membership with Creditreform  
   - Max. 25 employees **or** CHF 6 million annual turnover  
   - Claims between CHF 1,000 and CHF 25,000  
   - Max. 3 cases per year  
   - Documented order (delivery note or order confirmation)  
   - No relation to projects requiring building permits  

4. **Your Advantage**  
   - Protection against litigation costs  
   - Judicial pursuit without cost risk  
   - Greater decision-making security in debt recovery

## INFORMATION FOR DEBTORS (Received Payment Reminder from Debt Collection Agency)

---

### Received a Payment Reminder – What to Do?

1. **Payment for Legitimate Claims**  
   - Transfer the outstanding amount using the provided payment slip  
   - In case of financial difficulties: contact the debt collection agency, arrange installment payments  

2. **Lost Payment Slip**  
   - Request a new copy from the debt collection agency  

3. **Payment Already Made but Reminder Still Received**  
   - Possible overlap: briefly inform the debt collection agency about your payment  

**Further Information**: [inkassoverband.ch](https://www.inkassoverband.ch/)

---

### Objections and Complaints

1. **Unjustified Payment Reminder**  
   - Contact the debt collection agency directly to clarify the situation  

2. **Procedure for Objections**  
   - First complain to the debt collection agency  
   - If disagreement persists: complaint to the Ombudsman of Inkasso Suisse  
     - [Procedure for Objections (PDF)](https://inkassosuisse.ch/wp-content/uploads/2022/01/202214_Vorgehen-Beschwerden.pdf)  

3. **Complaint to Ombudsman**  
   - Written complaint after unsuccessful objection with the debt collection agency  
   - [Information about the Ombudsman](https://www.inkassoverband.ch/#kontakt)  
   - [Instructions (PDF)](https://inkassosuisse.ch/wp-content/uploads/2022/01/202214_Vorgehen-Beschwerden.pdf)  

**Information Required in Complaint**  
- Which member is the complaint against?  
- Description of the facts  
- Evidence (attachments numbered, table of contents)  
- Proof of your initial objection to the debt collection agency  

---

### Problems and Further Questions

1. **Incorrect or False Personal Information**  
   - Request correction directly from the debt collection agency  

2. **Payment Reminder Does Not Concern Me**  
   - Inform the debt collection agency to investigate the matter  

3. **Unable to Maintain Installment Payments**  
   - Contact the debt collection agency as early as possible to arrange new solutions  

---

### Default Damages and Additional Fees

1. **Default Damages**  
   - Costs incurred by the creditor due to delayed payment  
   - [Information on Default Damages (PDF)](https://inkassosuisse.ch/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/20221128-Glaeubigerschaden_extern.pdf)  

2. **Other Permissible Expenses**  
   - **Default Interest**: regulated by law or contract  
   - **Reminder Fees**: max. CHF 30.00 per reminder (total max. CHF 120.00)  
   - **Third-party Costs**: enforcement costs, credit checks, address inquiries, official fees, etc.  
   - **Installment Payment Surcharge**: max. CHF 20.00 per installment  
   - **Fee for Deletion of Debt Enforcement Record**: max. CHF 110.00 per enforcement (max. threefold)  

---

### Old Claims and Insolvency

1. **Old Loss Certificates**  
   - Statute of limitations after 20 years  
   - Any interruption extends the period  

2. **Insolvent – What Now?**  
   - Inform the debt collection agency about your situation  
   - Possible: deferment or reduction of installments  
   - Advice from **non-commercial debt counseling services**:  
     - [List of Debt Counseling Members (PDF)](https://schulden.ch/wp-content/uploads/2022/01/mitgliederliste-homepage-23122021.pdf)  

3. **Proof of Insolvency**  
   - Certificate from social welfare office or other documents (tax assessment, etc.)  
   - Member examines payment deferral according to Art. 4.2.11 CoC
